Global Trends & Challenges

Our world is undergoing rapid transformation. This page introduces the major trends shaping the next generation’s reality: seismic power shifts, technological breakthroughs and their misuse, societal inequality and the accelerating impacts of climate change. Use the tasks in the HUD to guide your journey and expand your understanding.

Global Power Shifts & New Risks

The next decade will be defined by seismic shifts in global power, pressure on liberal democracies, challenges to global governance, transformation of economic models and new uses and misuses of technology【14871036755630†L91-L103】. These trends demand a rethinking of how institutions function and how peace is kept, especially as geopolitical competition intensifies and emerging technologies blur the line between civilian and military domains.

Consider how these shifts might influence the stability of nations and the balance of power. Which alliances or conflicts could emerge, and how might they affect the ability of multilateral institutions to respond to crises?

Equality & Resilience

Research shows that more equal societies are better prepared to face future challenges. Equality is about more than wealth: it encompasses social inclusion, political participation and equal opportunities【14871036755630†L3430-L3458】. Achieving gender equality alone could create millions of jobs and raise global GDP, while policies like minimum wages or basic incomes have positive or neutral growth effects【14871036755630†L3430-L3458】.

Reflect on how equality strengthens resilience. How can we design institutions and policies that prioritise fair distribution of resources, equal representation and opportunities for all, and what lessons can we learn from societies that have successfully reduced inequality?

Climate & Health Impacts

Climate change will expose future generations to multiple, unequal, lifelong health problems, natural disasters and ecosystem destruction【158805180587571†L882-L906】. A child born in 2020 will experience seven times as many heat waves, twice as many droughts and wildfires and three times as many crop failures as someone born in 1960 unless we drastically cut emissions【712047896042378†L27-L52】.

Explore how climate mitigation and adaptation policies could reduce these impacts. What are the co‑benefits of climate action for health, economic development and social stability? How can we ensure that vulnerable communities are not left behind?

Digital Divides & AI Governance

The digital revolution offers immense opportunities yet threatens to deepen divides. The 2024 UN Summit of the Future adopted a pact whose annexes – the Declaration on Future Generations and the Global Digital Compact – aim to close digital divides, promote digital inclusion and establish responsible data and AI governance【471743158865615†L324-L394】. Proposals include a Global AI Foresight Network and an Earth Stewardship Initiative to embed ethical considerations into emerging technologies【471743158865615†L324-L394】.

Think about how unequal access to data, connectivity and AI expertise could exacerbate existing inequalities. How might inclusive digital policies, education and multi‑stakeholder governance ensure that technology benefits everyone?
